Methodology

How SourceRank extracts claims, verifies evidence, and computes credibility scores.

Score Formula

DimensionWeightWhat it measures
Accuracy Rate40%Share of resolved claims that were confirmed.
Prediction Score25%Accuracy on breaking, exclusive, and prediction claims.
Correction Behavior15%Penalty for refuted claims without a detected correction signal.
Source Quality10%Share of confirmations from configured tier-one sources.
Spam Index10%Penalty for unusually high original tweet volume.

Verdicts

VerdictMeaning
CONFIRMEDA relevant tier-one or corroborated source supports the claim.
REFUTEDA relevant tier-one source directly contradicts the claim.
UNVERIFIEDThe claim is still inside its verification window or evidence is too weak.
EXPIREDThe verification window passed without enough supporting evidence.

Rank Eligibility

Scores are computed for every active journalist, but leaderboard ranking requires a minimum number of resolved claims. This avoids presenting tiny samples as meaningful rankings. Rows below the threshold are marked N/R on the leaderboard.

Limitations

  • Current rankings are only as strong as local tweet coverage.
  • RSS and Google News evidence can miss stories that are not indexed or published in accessible feeds.
  • Contradiction detection is rule-based and intentionally conservative.
  • Scores are analytical estimates, not judgments of character or professional standing.
